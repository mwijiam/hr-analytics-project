# Key Insights - HR Attrition Analysis

## Summary
Out of 1,470 employees, 237 resigned, giving us a 16.12% attrition rate. This is above the typical 10-15% benchmark. The biggest drivers are overtime, low pay, poor work-life balance, and weak onboarding for new hires. Sales Representative is the most affected role.

---

## 1. Overall Attrition

- 237 out of 1,470 employees left (16.12%)
- Industry benchmark sits around 10-15%, so we're above normal
- At roughly $4,000 per hire, that's an estimated $948,000/year in replacement costs

---

## 2. High Risk Groups

| Category | Attrition Rate | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Sales Representative | 39.76% | Almost 4 in 10 leave, worst in the company |
| Laboratory Technician | 23.94% | Entry-level R&D role, likely outgrowing the position |
| Human Resources | 23.08% | Small team (63 people), each loss hits hard |
| Sales Executive | 17.48% | High pressure, commission-driven |

Employees in **Sales** have it the worst with a department-level attrition rate of 20.63%.

---

## 3. Key Factors

### Overtime
- With overtime: **30.5%** attrition
- Without overtime: **10.4%** attrition
- 3x higher risk. This is the single biggest red flag in the dataset.

### Job Satisfaction
- Low satisfaction (1/4): **22.8%** attrition
- Very high satisfaction (4/4): **11.2%** attrition
- Dissatisfied employees leave at roughly double the rate.

### Income
- Average monthly income of leavers: **$4,787**
- Average monthly income of stayers: **$6,832**
- A $2,045 gap. People earning under $3K/month have a 25.8% attrition rate.

### Work-Life Balance
- Bad WLB (1/4): **31.3%** attrition
- Good WLB (3-4/4): ~15% attrition
- Bad work-life balance doubles the quit rate.

### Tenure
- 0-1 years at company: **34.88%** attrition
- 10+ years at company: **8.13%** attrition
- First-year employees are 4x more likely to leave than long-tenured staff.

---

## 4. Things Worth Noting

1. **Overtime is a stronger predictor than salary.** Even well-paid employees leave if they're burned out.
2. **Age matters:** 18-24 year olds have 33% attrition. Younger employees explore more, which is partly natural, but onboarding quality matters.
3. **Single employees** resign at 25.5% vs married at 12.6%. Fewer anchors, more willingness to switch.
4. **Gender is roughly equal** (Male 17%, Female 14.8%). No obvious bias in this dataset.

---

## 5. Recommendations

### Quick Wins
1. **Cut unnecessary overtime.** Enforce max hours, hire extra hands in high-load teams.
2. **Fix onboarding.** Buddy system, 90-day check-ins, set clear expectations early.
3. **Salary review for <$3K earners.** A small raise is cheaper than replacing them.

### Longer Term
1. **Career pathing for Sales Reps.** Show a clear route to Sales Executive and beyond.
2. **Flexible work options.** Remote/hybrid for roles that allow it.
3. **Manager training.** Bad management drives more exits than bad pay.
4. **Quarterly satisfaction pulse surveys.** Catch issues before they become resignations.

---

## 6. What This Data Can't Tell Us

- **Why** exactly each person left (no exit interview data)
- Whether resignations were voluntary or pushed
- Market conditions, competitor offers, or personal reasons
- Quality of direct managers (only years-with-manager is available)
- This is a snapshot, not a trend over multiple years
